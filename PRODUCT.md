# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

MNS1 Express serves two public audiences:

- Experienced CDL-A drivers comparing pay, home time, equipment, dispatch quality, requirements, and whether the carrier is worth switching to.
- Shippers and operations teams looking for reliable Midwest dry van capacity from an asset-based carrier with direct accountability.

Most driver visitors are on mobile, often scanning quickly between loads, at home, or while comparing carriers. Shipper visitors need fast credibility signals, coverage clarity, and a direct way to reach a real person.

## Product Purpose

The public website should convert qualified CDL-A drivers and build shipper confidence without feeling like a generic trucking template. It exists to show that MNS1 is a serious Midwest carrier operating at fleet scale: family-owned, direct, fast, and accountable.

Success means drivers can understand pay, lanes, equipment, home time, requirements, and apply paths without friction. Shippers can understand capacity, coverage, and contact paths without digging.

## Brand Personality

Sharp, practical, and road-tested.

The voice should be direct and credible. It should feel like a real carrier with dispatch, trucks, drivers, freight, and a Plainfield terminal behind it. It should avoid recruiter hype, vague logistics language, and small-fleet brochure energy.

## Anti-references

- Generic AI landing pages with glass cards, decorative blobs, and vague hero claims.
- Overly beige SaaS layouts that could belong to any business.
- Small-carrier websites that look underfunded, abandoned, or patched by hand.
- Broker-style freight pages that obscure who owns the truck, who answers the phone, and who is accountable.
- Driver recruiting pages that promise everything but do not make pay, equipment, home time, and requirements easy to inspect.

## Design Principles

1. Make fleet scale visible early. The site should quickly communicate that MNS1 is a real 180+ truck operation, not a one-truck startup or broker facade.
2. Put action paths where intent appears. Driver pages should lead to apply and call actions; shipper pages should lead to quote and email actions.
3. Keep credibility concrete. Prefer real equipment, routes, locations, numbers, and policies over soft claims.
4. Build from shared source, not patched static output. Header, footer, CTA paths, route inventory, SEO, and content patterns should be maintainable in Astro source.
5. Design for mobile decisions first. Mobile visitors must be able to scan, navigate, and act without hidden or broken functionality.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA for public routes. Maintain semantic landmarks, one clear H1 per page, accessible link/button names, visible keyboard focus, reduced motion support where animation is used, readable mobile text, sufficient contrast for primary CTAs, and touch targets at or above 44px for mobile controls.
